History

The main street of every city or town has a story to tell.

This is the case of San José del Cabo where "La Calle Grande or Calle Ancha" was the recognized avenue of important events. The Calle Grande ran from north to south, beginning at the Casa Cural, today Casa de la Cultura, along Avenida Álvaro Obregón to the property of Mr. Esteban Green, known as “La Casa Atravesada” where today the continuation of the street is located. History

Many of the stories of this site occurred in the golden age of San José del Cabo (1900 -1940), a period of economic abundance generated by the production of sugar cane and tomato, later due to the fishing and processing of the liver of shark.

It is necessary to mention that in those times the isolation with the rest of the country was almost total.

Outstanding Facts

At the north end of the street was La Casa Cural, which was the home of Catholic priests, legend has it that there was a tunnel as a secret passageway between this building and the church of San José located in the center of town.

Construction of the Municipal Palace began in 1888, it was inaugurated until 1927 and the tower clock was placed in 1930.

La voz del Sur was the largest store in the town located in front of the Municipal Palace. These stores were owned by the brothers Valerio and Manuel González Canseco. This store was part of a telephone network that connected Casa Siete also located on La Calle Grande and a ranch in the Costa Azul Beach area.

Outstanding Facts

In this place the first cinemas were located, showing films from the Golden Age of the National Cinema.

One block from La Calle Grande was the soda fountain where a bottled soft drink called Jonrón was sold locally produced by Don Román Chiapa. This soft drink was distributed in stores and homes in the town.

In the surroundings of La Calle Grande, the public square, the kiosk, the church, businesses that ranged from a hairdresser, butcher, dentists and shops were located in what we now know as the historic center of San José del Cabo.
